Monuments of Madhya Pradesh

The Great Stupa at Sanchi

The Great Stupa at Sanchi is one of the oldest stone structures in India, and an important monument of Indian Architecture. It was originally commissioned by the Mauryan emperor Ashoka the Great in the 3rd century BCE. Its nucleus was a simple hemispherical brick structure built over the relics of the Buddha. It was crowned by the 'chhatra', a parasol-like structure symbolising high rank, which was intended to honour and shelter the relics. The original construction work of this stupa was overseen by Ashoka, whose wife Devi was the daughter of a merchant of nearby Vidisha.

The Khajuraho Group of Temples

Probably the most famous historical temples in the entirety of India, The Khajuraho Complex of Temples is a site you should not miss on your visit to Madhya Pradesh regardless of your area of interest. The complex encompasses numerous Hindu, Jain, and Buddhist temples which enjoy an unparalleled fame owing to their erotic carvings and sculptures. Khajuraho group of temples is a prominent site from both historical and cultural angle.

Gwalior Fort

This impenetrable fort is definitely one of the best historical places in Madhya Pradesh. The inscriptions, the monuments, and the architecture of the fort – each boasts an unprecedented diligence and charm. A visit to the fort will take you back in time when this culturally rich city was ruled by Tomars. Though there isn’t any concrete evidence as to when exactly the construction of the fort began, it is estimated that it was built around the 9th century.
